What is MalCommands?

MalCommands is a collection of commandline patterns often associated with cyber threat activity. Almost none of this is original work, I'm just trying to put it together in a more easily searchable and referenced format.

Why do this?

There are many resources documenting suspicious command-line activity across the Internet - this is designed to help aggregate information to a central repository, making it easy to search, learn from examples, infer based on MITRE Technique or Killchain, read references, etc.

How should I use it?

However you want - it was designed to be a broad research, reference and glossary tool - some use-cases may include the following;

  • Discovering tools or commandlines associated with a specific MITRE Technique or Tactic
  • Determining whether that weird commandline you saw matches any known-suspicious patterns or arguments.
  • Cross-referencing commandlines discovered in the course of a forensic investigation.
  • Finding new ways to use an existing tool for red-team objectives.
  • Better understanding the purpose or objective of a specific commandline or tool.
  • Understanding the risk or fidelity of a commandline with respect to environment consequences and detection techniques.
  • Finding additional references or uses for a specific command.
  • Determining known threat-actor attributions to certain tools or commandlines [WIP with References for each commandline]

You can't possibly map all commandline variations!

Correct - there is pretty much a limitless way to obfuscate, encode, encrypt or otherwise hide suspicious activity - cyber threats can and do change arguments, formats, etc at will when customizing offensive tools. Having said that, I have personally observed APTs pulling compiled tools straight from GitHub and using default arguments on a not-infrequent basis.
